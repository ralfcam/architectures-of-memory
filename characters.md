# Characters — The Architectures of Memory

Short reference for **who** appears across the series and **why** they matter to the argument. Not hagiography: include limits, controversies, and how each embodies a memory architecture problem.

---

## Technical line

### Frank Rosenblatt (1928–1971)

Psychologist; **Perceptron** (1957–1958) as learnable linear classifier with ambitious narrative. **Book 1:** optimism of the “thinking machine,” hardware (Mark I), media hype — setup for the limits *Perceptrons* later stressed.

### Marvin Minsky (1927–2016) & Seymour Papert (1928–2016)

*Perceptrons* (1969) — theoretical limits of single-layer linear threshold networks; contributed to connectionism’s winter narrative (alongside funding and other factors). **Book 1:** critique as **structural** not merely social.

### David E. Rumelhart (1942–2011)

Cognitive scientist; **PDP** / parallel distributed processing. **Book 1:** human-readable bridge from symbols to distributed representations.

### Geoffrey E. Hinton

Co-author of **backprop** popularization (Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams, 1986); decades of deep learning advocacy. **Books 1–4:** error propagation, representation learning, and the long arc to scale.

### Paul J. Werbos

PhD thesis (1974) on backprop in modern form — **priority and rediscovery** thread. **Book 1:** history of ideas vs. credit assignment.

### Sepp Hochreiter

**Diploma thesis (1991)** on vanishing gradients — the “unread proof” spine. **Books 1, 2, 5:** failure before fix; circle back in the coda.

### Jürgen Schmidhuber

Co-architect of **LSTM** line; **constant error flow** narrative. **Book 2:** from thesis to **Neural Computation (1997)** and beyond; credit and contested histories.

### Dzmitry Bahdanau, Kyunghyun Cho, Yoshua Bengio

**Neural machine translation** attention (Bahdanau et al., 2014). **Book 3:** attention before the Transformer.

### Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones, Aidan N. Gomez, Łukasz Kaiser, Illia Polosukhin

**“Attention Is All You Need” (2017).** **Book 3–4:** rupture from recurrence; scale and industrial uptake.

## Philosophy & letters

### Heraclitus (fragmentary)

**Flux** — identity across change. **Book 1:** river; what persists if the signal decays?

### Plutarch

**Ship of Theseus** (via Plutarch). **Book 1:** persistence of objects vs. institutions; replacement parts.

### Plato

**Meno** — is learning **recollection**? **Book 3:** retrieval vs. understanding; anamnesis vs. statistical pattern.

### Henri Bergson (1859–1941)

**Duration** (*la durée*) vs. spatialized time. **Book 1:** lived time vs. discrete steps in RNN unrolling.

### Jorge Luis Borges (1899–1986)

**Funes the Memorious** — total memory as incapacity. **Book 2:** selective forgetting as condition of thought.

### Andy Clark & David Chalmers

**Extended mind** (1998). **Book 3:** notebooks, phones, retrieval — where cognition stops.

### John R. Searle

**Chinese Room** (1980). **Book 4:** syntax vs. semantics at LLM scale — without sneering at either side.

### Paul Ricoeur (1913–2005)

**Memory, History, Forgetting** — narrative identity, forgiveness, **happy forgetting**. **Book 5:** ethics of memory work.

## Usage notes for writers

- **Introduce on first substantive mention** with one line of context (era, contribution).
- **Avoid winner’s history:** note parallel work (e.g., attention, backprop precursors) where it sharpens the *architecture* point.
- **Characters are lenses:** tie each back to **persistence, selection, or erasure** — the series’ tripod.
