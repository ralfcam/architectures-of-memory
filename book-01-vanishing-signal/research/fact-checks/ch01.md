## Item 1 — 1957 "Cornell Photoperceptron" proposal: archival reference

**Verdict: CONFIRMED with partial archival sourcing. Direct archival URL not publicly accessible; document identity confirmed from multiple secondary sources.**

The 1957 funding proposal is confirmed via the Wikipedia Mark I Perceptron article (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_I_Perceptron), which quotes directly from it:

> "Devices of this sort are expected ultimately to be capable of concept formation, language translation, collation of military intelligence, and the solution of problems through inductive logic."

The OAPEN Library PDF (*Perceptron*, library.oapen.org, 2025) confirms: "the Office of Naval Research (ONR) had funded Rosenblatt's Perceptron research beginning in July of 1957." The Smithsonian National Museum of American History object record (americanhistory.si.edu/collections/object/nmah_334414) further confirms the dual funding: "Office of Naval Research and the Rome Air Development Center."

**Confirmed facts for Ch.01:**
- The proposal predates the November 1958 *Psychological Review* paper by approximately 16 months.
- Funding from ONR commenced July 1957.
- No publicly accessible archival URL for the proposal document itself has been located. The Cornell digital library catalog record (digital.library.cornell.edu/catalog/ss:550351) holds the Mark I Perceptron image collection but does not appear to hold the proposal document itself.
- **⚠️ OPEN:** Primary archival location unresolved. The draft should attribute the Photoperceptron proposal quote to the Mark I Perceptron Wikipedia article as a secondary source, or note it as unverified from primary source.

## Item 2 — Pre-*Psychological Review* internal 1957 report: existence and catalogue reference

**Verdict: CONFIRMED. The 1957 internal CAL report is a distinct, identified document.**

The Studocu transcription reproduces the cover page of the original document:

> **Title:** THE PERCEPTRON: A PERCEIVING AND RECOGNIZING AUTOMATON (PROJECT PARA)  
> **Date:** January, 1957  
> **Author:** Frank Rosenblatt, Project Engineer  
> **Institution:** Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, Inc., Buffalo, New York  
> **Approver:** Alexander Stieber, Head, Air Defense Section, Systems Research Dept.

The ArXiv paper (arxiv.org/pdf/1810.04053) cites this as: Frank Rosenblatt, "The perceptron: A perceiving and recognizing automaton," Technical Report **85-460-1**, Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, 1957. The *Grey* journal (MIT Press, direct.mit.edu/grey) confirms: "Rosenblatt first published on the perceptron model in 1957 in a report for the Office of Naval Research titled *The Perceptron: A Perceiving and Recognizing Automaton*."

**Confirmed primary citation for Ch.01:**
> Frank Rosenblatt, "The Perceptron: A Perceiving and Recognizing Automaton (Project PARA)," Technical Report 85-460-1, Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, January 1957.

The November 1958 *Psychological Review* paper ("The Perceptron: A Probabilistic Model for Information Storage and Organization in the Brain," *Psychological Review* 65(6), pp. 386–408) is a subsequent expanded publication.

## Item 3 — 1958 NPL symposium: Rosenblatt in person vs. paper-only submission

**Verdict: PARTIALLY RESOLVED — symposium dates confirmed; Rosenblatt's attendance NOT definitively confirmed from available public sources.**

The Science Museum Group Collection (collection.sciencemuseumgroup.org.uk) and the India NISCPR OPAC record (opac.niscpr.res.in) confirm the proceedings:

- **Symposium:** "Mechanisation of Thought Processes"
- **Venue:** National Physical Laboratory, Teddington
- **Dates:** 24–27 November 1958
- **Published:** London: Her Majesty's Stationery Office, 1959. 2 vols., 980 pp. (Symposium No. 10)

The table of contents of the proceedings is not available in any consulted public-domain source. Whether Rosenblatt submitted a paper and/or attended in person cannot be confirmed without access to the volumes themselves.

**⚠️ UNRESOLVED — action for draft:** Change "presented at" to "submitted a paper to" contingent on confirming his paper appears in the proceedings, or remove the NPL attendance claim entirely. The proceedings are held physically at the Science Museum (London) and the National Physical Laboratory Library.

## Item 4 — NYT article: exact date and page number

**Verdict: CONFIRMED from two independent sources.**

The Cornell CS4780 course slides (cs.cornell.edu/courses/cs4780/2023fa) state: "Published: July 8, 1958 — *The New York Times*." The NYT archive record (nytimes.com/1958/07/08/archives/new-navy-device-learns-by-doing…) confirms: **July 8, 1958, Page 25**.

**Confirmed citation for Ch.01:**
> "New Navy Device Learns by Doing," *The New York Times*, July 8, 1958, p. 25.

The quoted phrase — "walk, talk, see, write, reproduce itself and be conscious of its existence" — is confirmed as from this article (cross-referenced by the Perceptron Wikipedia article). The press conference was a U.S. Navy event at which Rosenblatt spoke; the NYT article is the primary contemporary record.
