## Item 1 — Werbos 1974 thesis: text accessibility and derivation content

**Verdict: CONFIRMED. Full text is publicly accessible. Derivation present in August 1974 submitted version.**

Full text available as scanned PDF at gwern.net/doc/ai/nn/1974-werbos.pdf. Cover page confirms:

> **Title:** BEYOND REGRESSION: NEW TOOLS FOR PREDICTION AND ANALYSIS IN THE BEHAVIORAL SCIENCES  
> **Author:** Paul John Werbos  
> **Committee:** Applied Mathematics  
> **Degree:** Doctor of Philosophy in Statistics  
> **University:** Harvard University, Cambridge, Massachusetts  
> **Date:** August, 1974

Also published in expanded book form: Paul J. Werbos, *The Roots of Backpropagation: From Ordered Derivatives to Neural Networks and Political Forecasting*, Wiley-IEEE Press, 1994 (wiley-vch.de) — contains full 1974 thesis text plus three subsequent papers and a new introduction.

The Werbos 2004 paper (werbos.com/AD2004.pdf) confirms the derivation was developed during the thesis work: "The complete system in FORTRAN was almost ready for testing in mid-1974."

**Confirmed primary citation for Ch.03:**
> Paul J. Werbos, "Beyond Regression: New Tools for Prediction and Analysis in the Behavioral Sciences," Ph.D. dissertation, Committee on Applied Mathematics, Harvard University, August 1974.

The 1975 date appearing in some secondary citations is the ProQuest/UMI registration year, not the submission date. **Use 1974 consistently in the draft.**

**⚠️ NOTE:** gwern.net hosts a copy; authoritative repository is ProQuest Dissertations. Wiley-IEEE 1994 is the only commercial publication of the full text.

## Item 2 — Linnainmaa 1970: precedence and Rumelhart awareness

**Verdict: PRECEDENCE CONFIRMED. RUMELHART AWARENESS: CONFIRMED AS ABSENT (independently derived). Historiographically resolved.**

Seppo Linnainmaa's 1970 University of Helsinki master's thesis is the first derivation of reverse-mode automatic differentiation. Schmidhuber (people.idsia.ch/~juergen/who-invented-backpropagation-2014.html): "Explicit, efficient error backpropagation (BP) in arbitrary, discrete, possibly sparsely connected, NN-like networks apparently was first described in a 1970 master's thesis (Linnainmaa, 1970, 1976), **albeit without reference to NNs**." An English paper was published in 1976 but, per Yuxi Liu (yuxi-liu-wired.github.io/essays/posts/backstory-of-backpropagation/, 2023): "He has priority but no paternity."

On Rumelhart awareness: the Wikipedia Backpropagation article (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation) is unambiguous: "Around 1982, David E. Rumelhart independently developed backpropagation and taught the algorithm to others in his research circle. **He did not cite previous work as he was unaware of them.**" This resolves the ARC-3 open question definitively.

**Key distinctions for Ch.03:**
- **Linnainmaa (1970):** Reverse-mode AD, in Finnish, not framed in NN terms. Priority without neural-network application.
- **Werbos (1974):** Backpropagation applied explicitly to multi-layer NNs. First neural-network backprop.
- **Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams (1986):** Backprop made accessible and empirically demonstrated at scale. The paper that operationalised the algorithm for the ML community.

The draft should present all three as genuinely independent derivations and resist flattening them into a single discovery.

## Item 3 — Parker 1985 MIT TR-47: date and relationship to Rumelhart *Nature* submission

**Verdict: CONFIRMED. Parker TR-47 dated February 1985. Rumelhart and Parker worked simultaneously and independently.**

The gwern.net PDF (gwern.net/doc/ai/nn/1985-parker.pdf) confirms the cover page:

> **Title:** Learning-Logic: Casting the Cortex of the Human Brain in Silicon  
> **Author:** David B. Parker  
> **Report:** Technical Report TR-47, **February 1985**  
> **Institution:** Center for Computational Research in Economics and Management Science, MIT

Rumelhart timeline (Wikipedia Backpropagation):
- **~1982:** Rumelhart independently derives backpropagation; unaware of Werbos or Linnainmaa.
- **1985:** Rumelhart publishes a 1985 ICS Report (month unconfirmed — see flag below).
- **October 9, 1986:** Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams, "Learning representations by back-propagating errors," *Nature*, 323, pp. 533–536.

**⚠️ OPEN:** The precise month of Rumelhart's 1985 ICS Report relative to Parker's February 1985 TR needs verification from the UCSD ICS technical report series. The mutual-awareness question for the 1985 reports (as opposed to the 1982 derivation) remains unresolved from public sources.

## Item 4 — PDP Research Group: membership roster

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED. Core membership documented from chapter attributions; full roster requires PDP Vol. 1 preface p. xi.**

The edX/Cambridge paper ("Attaining landmark status: Rumelhart and McClelland's PDP") confirms the group's formation: "They also led the formation of a discussion group at UCSD... This group, which became known as the Parallel Distributed Processing Group, included a number of their colleagues who eventually became contributors to the [volumes]" (citing Rumelhart, McClelland, et al., 1986, p. xi).

**Core members identifiable from chapter attributions in secondary literature:**
- David E. Rumelhart (co-editor, Vol. 1)
- James L. McClelland (co-editor, Vols. 1 & 2)
- Geoffrey E. Hinton
- Ronald J. Williams
- Michael I. Jordan
- Terrence J. Sejnowski
- David Zipser
- Alan Kawamoto
- Jerome A. Feldman
- Norman H. Packard

**⚠️ PARTIALLY UNRESOLVED:** The complete authoritative roster is in the preface of Rumelhart, McClelland et al. (1986), p. xi. The draft should cite that page for the full list and note the group comprised approximately 15–20 researchers at UCSD and affiliated institutions.

**Confirmed citation for Ch.03:**
> David E. Rumelhart, James L. McClelland, and the PDP Research Group, *Parallel Distributed Processing: Explorations in the Microstructure of Cognition*, 2 vols., MIT Press, 1986.
