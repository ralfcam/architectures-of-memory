## Findings

### 1. Bengio et al. 1994 — IEEE TNN full citation and 1993 TR relationship

**Full citation confirmed from multiple primary sources:**

> Bengio, Y., Simard, P., and Frasconi, P. “Learning Long-Term Dependencies with Gradient Descent is Difficult.” *IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks*, Vol. 5, No. 2, March 1994, pp. 157–166. DOI: **10.1109/72.279181**. PMID: 18267787.

The HKBU PDF of the paper (web:519) reproduces the header: “IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON NEURAL NETWORKS, VOL. 5, NO. 2, MARCH 1994”, confirming volume, issue, and month. PubMed (web:96) confirms: *IEEE Trans Neural Netw.* 1994;5(2):157–66, DOI 10.1109/72.279181, PMID 18267787. The University of Florence FLORE repository (web:887) confirms: volume 5(2), pages 157–166, ISSN 1045-9227. Semantic Scholar (web:97) independently confirms: volume 5, issue 2, pages 157–66.

**The 1993 technical report relationship:**

The paper is frequently encountered with a 1993 date in citation databases because the work circulated as a technical report at Université de Montréal in 1993 before journal publication in March 1994. Bengio's own 2016 ICML slide deck (web:329) cites the paper as “Y. Bengio, P. Simard & P. Frasconi, IEEE Trans. Neural Nets, 1994” without referencing a prior TR, indicating the canonical citation is 1994.

**CRITICAL CLARIFICATION:** The chapter must cite the 1994 IEEE TNN publication, not a 1993 technical report. The 1993 TR predates peer review and should not be cited as a primary source. If an internal report citation is needed, it must be labelled as such with the Montreal institution. The authoritative citable record is the March 1994 IEEE TNN paper, DOI 10.1109/72.279181.

**Verdict:** Citation fully confirmed. Vol. 5, No. 2, March 1994, pp. 157–166. DOI: 10.1109/72.279181. PMID: 18267787. ISSN: 1045-9227. Three authors: Yoshua Bengio, Patrice Simard, Paolo Frasconi.

### 2. Waibel TDNN — 1987 vs. 1989: two distinct publications

**CRITICAL CORRECTION — there are two separate TDNN papers that must not be conflated:**

**Publication A — 1987/1989 IEEE TASLP (the canonical TDNN paper):**

Dblp (web:892) records the authoritative citation:

> Waibel, Alexander; Hanazawa, Toshiyuki; Hinton, Geoffrey E.; Shikano, Kiyohiro; Lang, Kevin J. “Phoneme recognition using time-delay neural networks.” *IEEE Transactions on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing*, 37(3): 328–339 (1989).

This is the journal publication of the TDNN work. The mbrenndoerfer.com article (web:331) states Waibel “introduced” TDNNs “in 1987” at CMU, referring to the initial technical report or conference version. The 1987 date corresponds to internal CMU work or a conference abstract; the peer-reviewed, citable publication is the **1989 IEEE TASLP** journal paper.

**Publication B — NeurIPS 1989 (Hampshire & Waibel):**

The NeurIPS 1989 paper (web:338) is a **distinct** paper by different authors:

> Hampshire, John B. II, and Alex Waibel. “Connectionist Architectures for Multi-Speaker Phoneme Recognition.” *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 2* (NeurIPS 1989), pp. 203–210.

This is not the original TDNN paper — it is a follow-on multi-speaker extension using TDNN-based architectures. The chapter must not cite this as the first TDNN publication.

**CRITICAL CORRECTION for Ch.16:** The first peer-reviewed TDNN publication is Waibel et al., *IEEE Trans. Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing*, 37(3): 328–339, **1989**. The “1987” figure refers to CMU internal/technical report work, which is not independently verifiable from sources consulted here. The chapter must use the 1989 IEEE TASLP citation as the primary reference, and may note in a footnote that the architecture was developed at CMU from 1987.

> ⚠️ **Flagged — OPEN QUESTION:** The 1987 CMU technical report or conference presentation by Waibel introducing TDNNs has not been independently located and confirmed with a specific title, venue, and date. The mbrenndoerfer.com source gives 1987 without a primary citation. If the chapter wishes to cite the 1987 origin, a primary CMU technical report reference must be located.  
> Suggested query: `"Waibel 1987 CMU technical report time delay neural network speech recognition original TR number Carnegie Mellon"`

**Full confirmed citation for Ch.16:**
> Waibel, A., Hanazawa, T., Hinton, G., Shikano, K., and Lang, K. J. “Phoneme Recognition Using Time-Delay Neural Networks.” *IEEE Transactions on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing*, 37(3): 328–339, 1989.

### 3. Chen & Billings 1990 NARX — full citation and Narendra relationship

**Full citation confirmed from Semantic Scholar and University of Southampton ePrints:**

Semantic Scholar (web:888) records:

> Chen, Sheng; Billings, Stephen A.; Grant, P. M. “Non-linear system identification using neural networks.” *International Journal of Control*, Vol. 51, 1990, pp. 1191–1214.

The University of Southampton ePrints record (web:890) confirms: Chen, S., Billings, S.A. and Grant, P.M. (1990), *International Journal of Control*, 51, 1191–1214. The White Rose ePrints record (web:893) records a 1989 **Research Report** version (University of Sheffield), which is the pre-publication technical report preceding the 1990 journal paper — confirming the same pattern as Bengio 1993/1994.

**IMPORTANT — Three authors, not two:**

The issue body cites “S. Chen and S. A. Billings” as authors. The Semantic Scholar and Southampton records both include a **third author: P. M. Grant**. The full citation must read: Chen, S., Billings, S.A., and Grant, P.M. (1990).

**Issue number not confirmed:**

Semantic Scholar records volume 51 and pages 1191–1214 but does not specify issue number 6. The issue number (51(6)) cited in the ARC-36 issue body appears in the Lin et al. (NeurIPS 1996) citation list, which is a secondary source. This needs direct journal verification.

> ⚠️ **Flagged — OPEN QUESTION:** *International Journal of Control* volume 51, issue number for the Chen, Billings & Grant paper. The issue number “6” comes from Lin et al. (1996) as a secondary citation. Confirm against Taylor & Francis journal archive or DOI.  
> Suggested query: `"Chen Billings Grant Non-linear system identification neural networks International Journal Control 1990 volume 51 issue 6 DOI Taylor Francis 10.1080"`

**The Narendra (1990) relationship:**

Kumpati S. Narendra and Kannan Parthasarathy, “Identification and Control of Dynamical Systems Using Neural Networks,” *IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks*, 1(1): 4–27, 1990 — is the companion foundational NARX paper cited alongside Chen & Billings in Lin et al. (1996). These are **parallel, independent** contributions to neural NARX modelling: Chen & Billings focus on discrete-time nonlinear identification; Narendra & Parthasarathy frame the problem in terms of dynamical systems control. Neither paper cites the other as prior work. The chapter should treat them as parallel 1990 foundations, not as a parent–child relationship.

**Verdict for Ch.16:** Cite as Chen, S., Billings, S.A., and **Grant, P.M.** (1990). International Journal of Control, 51, 1191–1214. Issue number “6” flagged for direct journal verification. Narendra & Parthasarathy (1990) IEEE TNN 1(1):4–27 is the parallel foundational paper.

## Narrative threads for Ch.16

1. **The TR–journal lag as institutional memory gap:** Both Bengio (1993 TR → 1994 TNN) and Chen & Billings (1989 Research Report → 1990 IJC) follow the same pattern: a year of invisible institutional circulation before the work enters the citable record. This gap is the book’s thesis in miniature — what an institution “knows” before it “knows it officially.”
2. **The TDNN’s double identity:** The 1987 CMU internal work and the 1989 IEEE TASLP journal paper are the same architecture at two stages of institutional recognition. The architecture existed before it had a citation — a case of memory without record.
3. **Parallel 1990 foundations (Chen/Billings and Narendra):** Two independent groups simultaneously formalised the NARX framework in 1990 without citing each other. This is convergent discovery — the field’s memory of sequential systems crystallising simultaneously in two labs.
4. **The LSTM DOI as canonical anchor:** The DOI 10.1162/neco.1997.9.8.1735 is one of the most-cited DOIs in machine learning. Its stability over nearly three decades while the Springer reprint circulates in parallel illustrates how institutional memory (the journal of record) and informal memory (reprints, preprints) coexist and compete.
5. **Frasconi as the vanishing author:** Paolo Frasconi is the third author of the Bengio 1994 paper and a PhD student at the time. He is almost never named in popular accounts, which cite “Bengio et al.” The chapter can use this as a case study in how authorship memory is compressed over time.
